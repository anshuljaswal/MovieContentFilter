1. Load general filter

Please open a general filter (usually ".mcf") for a movie or TV show. That filter will be customized so that you can download your personal filter at the end. You may either open a ".mcf" file and copy its content to the large text field below, or you may choose one of our existing filters from the list. If you cannot find your movie yet, why not try to create your first own filter today? :) We will be happy to help you!

2. Synchronization

Having imported the general filter's content, the start and end time of the film source should have been added below. The start and end time are displayed as seen by the creators of the general filter. Please check if this matches with your movie source. Why is this necessary? Because movies are distributed in multiple versions, some shorter and some longer, and some versions even differ in playback speed. For example, one version of your favorite film may be 1.05× the speed of another version. Our filters can only be applied correctly if the start and end times are synchronized. Most commonly, you do not have to make any significant changes.

3. Do you use VLC media player?

If you use VLC media player to play your videos, please enter the path ("URI") to the film source on your device. For convenience, we have added a standard location that would play the file "Movie.mp4" from whatever directory you later open your personal filter in.

Learn how to play from hard drive or DVD or web or Blu-ray.

4. Preferences
5. Download

As the final step before watching, you can download your personal filter file below. The filter is available for the two popular video players "VLC media player" and "MPlayer" each. Please store the downloaded file on your device. It is a very small file that will control the playback of your actual movie content. Having downloaded your personal filter file, just open it with the designated video player software and enjoy your movie.

If you have adjusted the filter preferences above as desired, you may download your personal filter file now. Happy watching!
